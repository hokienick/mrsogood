# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Small business owners (dentists, restaurant owners, salon owners, contractors) in San Diego and nationally. They are not designers. Most arrive after Nick cold-emails them a free 24-hour redesign mockup of their own site, or via word of mouth. They are skimming on a phone between customers, deciding in under a minute whether Nick is legit and worth replying to. The job to be done: "convince me this guy can make MY business look this good, and that saying yes is low-risk."

## Product Purpose

Portfolio and marketing site for Mr. So Good, Nick's solo web design brand. It exists to convert mockup recipients and inbound visitors into paying clients ($600 flat redesign). Success: the visitor looks at the before/after work, believes the transformation is real, and submits the contact form. The site itself is the strongest proof of skill, so its design quality IS the sales pitch.

## Brand Personality

Confident, personable, bold. A talented individual you'd want to hire, not an agency. Energetic with editorial personality; Nick is a small business owner himself and the voice should feel like a real person who gets it. Emotions to evoke: trust, excitement ("my site could look like THIS"), and ease (no-risk offer, no hard feelings).

## Anti-references

- Generic AI/SaaS template: Inter everywhere, purple gradients, identical icon-card grids, eyebrow labels above every section.
- Corporate agency site: stocky, safe, "we synergize your brand" energy. This brand is one person, not a team page with headshots.
- The cream/sand AI default: the current body bg (#F5F0E8) sits squarely in the 2026 warm-neutral AI band and should be rethought; warmth belongs in the accent, type, and imagery, not a parchment body.
- Overdesigned portfolio art piece: experimental scroll-jacking that impresses designers but confuses a dentist looking for a website.
- Positive reference: savagemke.com (editorial, artsy, opinionated) — for energy, not for copying.

## Design Principles

1. Practice what you preach: every section must be evidence that Nick can design. If a section would look at home in a template marketplace, it fails.
2. The work sells, the site assists: before/after transformations are the hero content; everything else clears a path to them and to the form.
3. Bold but legible: editorial personality and rich motion, never at the cost of a non-designer understanding the page in five seconds.
4. One person, real voice: copy and design should feel hand-made by a confident individual. No em-dashes, no AI-sounding copy, no corporate plural.
5. Low-risk by design: the free 24-hour mockup offer should be impossible to miss and frictionless to act on.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA: body text ≥4.5:1 contrast, large text ≥3:1, keyboard navigable, visible focus states. Motion is a core part of the brand and should stay rich and creative, but every animation needs a prefers-reduced-motion alternative (already partially in place via the global reduced-motion kill switch in index.css). Mobile-first; most visitors arrive on phones.
